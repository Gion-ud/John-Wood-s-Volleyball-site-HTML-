Volleyball

Volleyball is a dynamic and engaging team sport played by millions of people around the world. It involves two teams, usually of six players each, trying to score points by sending a ball over a net and grounding it on the opponent’s court. The sport emphasizes agility, teamwork, and strategy, and is known for its fast-paced and exciting rallies.

Invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Massachusetts, USA, volleyball has evolved into both a recreational pastime and a professional, competitive sport. Today, volleyball is played in many forms, including indoor, beach, sitting (for para-athletes), and even snow volleyball in some parts of the world.

Key Skills Needed

  • Serving: Starting the play by sending the ball over the net from behind the baseline.
  • Passing: Receiving the serve or the opponent’s attack and accurately directing the ball to a teammate.
  • Setting: Usually the second touch, used to position the ball for a hitter.
  • Hitting (spiking): Attacking the ball aggressively to try to score.
  • Blocking: Preventing the opponent's attack from crossing the net.
  • Digging: Defensive skill to prevent the ball from touching the ground after a strong attack.

Basic Rules

  1. A match is typically played as best-of-five sets.
  2. Each set is played to 25 points (15 in the fifth set), and a team must win by 2 points.
  3. A team can touch the ball up to three times before sending it over the net.
  4. The same player cannot hit the ball twice in a row.
  5. The ball must not be caught, lifted, or thrown.
  6. The game begins with a serve from behind the baseline.

Famous Volleyball Nations

Did You Know?

  • The first volleyball was made from the bladder of a basketball.
  • Volleyball became an Olympic sport in 1964.
  • The net height is 2.43m for men and 2.24m for women.
  • Beach volleyball became an Olympic event in 1996.
  • Libero players wear a different-colored jersey and specialize in defense.
